# Landing Page Build Brief — B2B Consulting Leads SaaS

Use this document as the source brief for designing and building a single-page landing page for a SaaS product that sells curated consulting leads to business consulting firms.

## Project Goal

Create a premium, calm, conversion-focused landing page that explains a very simple promise:

**Consulting firms receive verified, curated lead lists every week without having to manually search for prospects.**

The page should feel high-trust and editorial, not flashy startup SaaS. The design direction should closely follow the attached reference image:

- Warm off-white background.
- Elegant serif headline paired with clean sans-serif supporting text.
- Minimal, premium spacing.
- Black/dark charcoal sections used selectively for contrast.
- Soft beige and muted gold/orange accents.
- Subtle shadows, thin borders, rounded cards.
- Mostly left-aligned content outside the hero.
- No loud gradients, no bright SaaS blues, no generic icon-circle feature blocks.

Think: refined boutique B2B software, calm confidence, understated luxury.

## Product Summary

This product is a B2B SaaS tool for **business consulting firms**. Customers subscribe to receive **weekly curated lead lists** based on their target consulting category, geography, and company size.

At signup, the customer selects a plan and fills in a short preference form once. After that, they receive a weekly email with a downloadable lead file. There is **no dashboard and no search UI in the MVP**.

The core value proposition is convenience plus relevance:

- No manual LinkedIn prospecting.
- No list building from scratch.
- No messy or unverified data.
- Just relevant companies and decision-makers delivered weekly.

## Audience

Primary audience:

- IT consulting firms
- HR consulting firms
- Finance consulting firms
- Legal consulting firms
- Operations / supply chain consultants
- Strategy consultants
- Cybersecurity consultants
- ESG / sustainability consultants

These buyers need a steady pipeline of potential clients for outbound outreach.

## Core Message

The homepage should communicate these ideas clearly:

1. Get fresh consulting leads delivered every week.
2. Leads are filtered to match your niche, geography, and target company size.
3. Emails are verified before delivery.
4. Setup is simple: subscribe once, set preferences once, receive leads weekly.
5. This is an MVP service product, so simplicity is a strength.

## Suggested Brand Positioning

Use positioning that feels specific and believable. Avoid inflated claims like “revolutionary” or “AI-powered growth engine” unless the implementation clearly supports that.

Preferred framing:

- Weekly lead delivery for consulting firms.
- Curated, verified outbound-ready company and contact lists.
- Built for boutique and mid-sized consulting teams that need a repeatable pipeline.
- A simpler alternative to manual prospecting.

Possible headline directions:

- Find better consulting leads. Win more client conversations.
- Stop building lead lists manually. Start reaching the right companies.
- Verified consulting leads, delivered weekly.
- Set your targeting once. Receive outbound-ready leads every week.

Possible subheadline direction:

- Curated company and decision-maker lists for consulting firms, filtered by specialty, geography, and company size, then delivered to your inbox every week.

## Product Mechanics To Reflect On The Page

The landing page should explain a simple 3-step flow:

1. Choose a plan.
2. Set your consulting preferences.
3. Receive verified weekly lead files.

The onboarding preference inputs are:

- Consulting type / specialty.
- Target region or country.
- Target company size.

Delivery format:

- Weekly email.
- Download link to CSV, Excel, or CRM-ready file depending on plan.
- Signed URL delivery instead of attachment.

## Key Proof Points

Use these throughout the page in restrained ways:

- Verified leads every week.
- Target bounce rate below 2%.
- Leads delivered based on saved preferences.
- CSV / Excel / CRM-ready outputs depending on plan.
- No dashboard needed for MVP.
- Includes professional data points such as name, title, company, email, and on higher tiers LinkedIn URL, phone, company size, tech stack, and trigger events.

Do not overdo numeric metrics if they cannot be visually substantiated in the page design.

## Pricing Information

Include a pricing section with three tiers:

### Starter

- $79 / month
- 25 verified leads per week
- 1 category
- Country-level geography filter
- CSV export
- Includes name, title, company, email

### Growth

- $149 / month
- 75 verified leads per week
- 3 categories
- Country + region filter
- CSV + Excel export
- Includes LinkedIn URL and company size

### Pro

- $299 / month
- 200 verified leads per week
- All categories
- Country + region + city filter
- CSV + Excel + CRM-ready export
- Includes phone, tech stack, recent trigger event, one on-demand top-up per month

## Content Sections

Structure the landing page roughly like this:

### 1. Header

- Minimal wordmark or logotype.
- Nav items can be simple: Product, How it works, Pricing, FAQ.
- Top-right CTA button: Request access, Join waitlist, or Book a demo.
- Keep header slim and premium.

### 2. Hero

- Large serif headline with part of the message highlighted in muted gold/orange italics, similar to the reference image.
- Supporting paragraph centered under the headline.
- Primary CTA button plus a secondary text/button action.
- Optional small trust strip below hero (examples of consulting specialties or delivery/output benefits).

### 3. Social Proof / Trust Band

Instead of generic logos if none are available, use one of these alternatives:

- A muted strip naming consulting verticals served.
- A line such as “Built for IT, HR, Finance, Legal, and Operations consulting teams.”
- Or a compact row of target audience categories.

If logos are used, they must be clearly placeholder-free and credible.

### 4. Product Overview Section

Introduce the product as a “lead delivery system” or “weekly lead command centre” for consulting firms.

This section should visually show a mock product surface such as:

- A clean table preview of leads.
- Columns like name, title, company, email, location, verified date.
- A summary row or KPI strip above it.

The UI mockup should feel like a lightweight service dashboard preview, even though the MVP itself does not have a real dashboard.

### 5. Proof / Testimonial Section

Follow the reference image’s dark section treatment.

Use this section to present:

- A testimonial-style quote from a hypothetical consulting team persona, OR
- A narrative proof block about time saved and lead quality, OR
- A “why teams switch from manual prospecting” section.

Tone should be believable and grounded. Avoid fake enterprise chest-thumping.

### 6. How It Works

Use a refined 3-step or 4-step editorial layout.

Recommended steps:

1. Define your consulting niche.
2. Set geography and company size.
3. Receive verified weekly lead files.
4. Start outreach with cleaner data.

Each step can pair short copy with a UI card or email/file mockup.

### 7. Categories / Use Cases

Show who it is for, such as:

- IT & Digital Transformation
- HR & Talent Consulting
- Finance & Accounting
- Legal & Compliance
- Operations & Supply Chain
- Strategy & Management
- Cybersecurity
- ESG & Sustainability

This can be done as an elegant grid or editorial list, not colorful icon cards.

### 8. Pricing

Display three clear pricing cards or columns. Keep styling restrained and readable.

One plan can be visually emphasized, but avoid flashy “most popular” SaaS clichés unless done tastefully.

### 9. FAQ

Answer practical buyer questions such as:

- What kind of leads are included?
- How are emails verified?
- How does delivery work?
- Is there a dashboard?
- Which regions are supported at launch?
- Can preferences be updated later?

### 10. Final CTA

Echo the reference image structure:

- Large serif closing statement.
- Highlighted italic phrase in muted gold/orange.
- Simple CTA button.
- Keep the section spacious and calm.

### 11. Footer

Simple footer with:

- Brand name.
- Short one-line description.
- Product links.
- Company links.
- Legal links.
- CTA button repeated at right if desired.

## Design Direction

The page design should be strongly inspired by the attached screenshot.

### Visual Style

- Background: warm ivory / soft beige.
- Typography: elegant serif for major headings, modern sans-serif for body and interface text.
- Accent: muted amber / burnt gold for highlighted words and tiny detail accents.
- Dark section: deep brown-black instead of pure black.
- Borders: thin, soft, neutral.
- Shadows: subtle and warm.
- Radius: medium, not overly rounded.
- Layout: clean sections with generous whitespace and restrained motion.

### Tone Keywords

- Editorial
- High-trust
- Premium
- Calm
- Intelligent
- Minimal
- Structured

### What To Avoid

- Bright blue SaaS palette.
- Loud AI visuals.
- Generic abstract blobs.
- Heavy gradients.
- Overuse of icons.
- Overcrowded comparison tables in the hero.
- Overclaiming language.
- Anything that makes the product look like a generic growth-hacking tool.

## UX Notes

This product is intentionally simple at MVP stage, so the page should sell clarity and convenience rather than software complexity.

The user should quickly understand:

- Who it is for.
- What they receive.
- How often they receive it.
- How targeting works.
- What the plans cost.
- Why it saves time versus manual prospecting.

Primary CTA ideas:

- Request access
- Join waitlist
- Book a demo
- Get started

Choose one primary conversion path and use it consistently.

## Copy Guidance

Use concise, polished copy. Short paragraphs. No buzzword overload.

Good copy qualities:

- Specific.
- Operationally credible.
- Calm and direct.
- Buyer-oriented.

Bad copy qualities:

- “10x your pipeline overnight”
- “Revolutionary AI lead engine”
- “Dominate your market instantly”
- “Unlock next-gen outbound transformation”

## Technical Notes For The Builder

- Build as a single responsive landing page.
- Desktop-first elegance, but fully mobile-friendly.
- Use semantic HTML.
- Keep interactions subtle.
- Light mode should be primary; dark mode support is optional unless requested.
- Mockups should look realistic and product-specific.
- If using imagery, keep it abstract/product-led rather than stock-photo heavy.

## Deliverable

Produce a landing page that feels like a premium vertical SaaS homepage for consulting lead delivery, using the product details from the product brief and the visual language of the attached reference.
